# VerseDrop Community Guidelines

**Last updated:** January 1, 2026

VerseDrop is a place to share Scripture with the people around you — strangers, neighbors, anyone who happens to walk past where you've been. To keep it a place worth being, we ask everyone to follow these guidelines. Violations can result in content removal, temporary suspension, or a permanent ban.

These guidelines are part of our [Terms of Service](./terms-of-service.md). Where the two overlap, the Terms of Service control.

---

## The spirit of VerseDrop

VerseDrop is about **leaving encouragement in physical spaces**. Drop a verse where it might mean something to whoever finds it. Pick one up and let it sit with you for a moment. Leave a kind word in return.

What it is not: a soapbox for political fights, a marketing channel, a platform for harassment, or a way to track or target specific people.

---

## What we encourage

- **Thoughtful drops** in places where strangers might appreciate a moment of Scripture — parks, trails, hospital waiting rooms, coffee shops, transit stops
- **Kind, brief notes** when you discover a verse ("Found this on a hard day — thank you")
- **Honest reactions** with Amen, Heart, or Pray
- **Reporting** drops or notes that violate these guidelines
- **Blocking** users whose content you don't want to see
- **Choosing safe, accessible, public locations** when you drop

---

## What's not allowed

### Hate, harassment, threats

No content that:
- Attacks, dehumanizes, or incites violence against people based on **race, ethnicity, national origin, religion, gender, gender identity, sexual orientation, disability, age, immigration status, or any other protected characteristic**
- Threatens, intimidates, or harasses any specific person or group
- Uses slurs, dehumanizing comparisons, or hateful symbols

This applies even if you're "quoting" or "joking." If a reasonable person would read it as an attack, it's an attack.

### Sexual or graphic content

No sexually explicit content, no graphic violence, no shock content. VerseDrop is a general-audience app.

### Personal information

No phone numbers, addresses, full legal names, license plates, or other information that identifies a private individual — yours or anyone else's. Don't post photos of identifiable people without their consent (though VerseDrop does not currently support image uploads, this rule extends to anything you type).

### Off-topic and spam

No commercial promotion, advertising, link shortening, affiliate links, fundraising, political campaigning, MLM recruitment, or coordinated posting. Drops should be about Scripture, not your side project or your cause.

### Harmful, illegal, or dangerous behavior

No content that:
- Promotes self-harm, suicide, or eating disorders
- Promotes illegal drugs, weapons, or violence
- Encourages dangerous physical actions ("walk into traffic to get this verse")
- Drops verses in genuinely unsafe locations (active construction sites, in roadways, private property where trespass is likely)
- Doxes, stalks, or surveils any person
- Constitutes or facilitates fraud, scams, or theft

### Misrepresentation

- Don't claim to be someone you're not
- Don't impersonate VerseDrop staff, churches, charities, or other organizations
- Don't fabricate "found this drop" notes for drops you actually created yourself

### Misuse of Scripture

We're a Bible-verse app. We are not the Bible police. But:
- Quote KJV verses accurately when using the built-in search (other translations are welcome in custom messages but should be cited honestly)
- Don't use verses as cover for content that would otherwise be prohibited (a verse plus a slur is still a slur)
- Don't strip context to weaponize a verse against a specific person

### Platform abuse

- No bots, scrapers, or automated tooling
- No attempts to bypass moderation, content filters, blocks, or rate limits
- No multiple accounts created to evade a ban or to game reactions/badges
- No reverse engineering or attempts to access systems you're not authorized to access

## How moderation works

### Automatic filters

When you create a drop with a custom message, or post a note, our automated filter checks for prohibited language and obvious spam patterns. Content the filter flags may be:

- **Allowed** if it appears clean
- **Held for review** if it contains links, email addresses, or other ambiguous signals
- **Rejected** if it contains content from our blocklist

### User reports

If you see something that violates these guidelines, tap the drop and select **Report drop**. Choose the most accurate reason:

- **Spam** — Promotional, off-topic, or repeated content
- **Offensive content** — Hate, slurs, sexual content, graphic violence
- **Harassment** — Targeted attacks on a specific person or group
- **Inappropriate** — Other content that doesn't belong in a general-audience app
- **Other** — Anything else; include details in the description field

You can also tap **Block user** to hide all drops from a specific user.

### Auto-hide threshold

When a drop receives **3 reports from different users**, it is automatically hidden pending manual review. This protects the community from coordinated abuse without giving any single user veto power.

### Manual review and escalation

Our team reviews flagged content as quickly as we can. Reviewers consider:
- The text and location of the drop or note
- The reason and details provided in reports
- The reporting user's history
- The author's history

Reviewers can: restore the content, leave it hidden, issue a warning to the author, suspend the author temporarily, or permanently ban the author.

For urgent situations — credible threats, imminent harm, child safety concerns — email **safety@versedrop.app** with as much detail as you can provide. We escalate these immediately and cooperate with law enforcement when appropriate.

---

## Enforcement ladder

We try to be proportional. First-time, low-severity violations usually get a warning and content removal. Repeated or severe violations escalate.

| Violation tier | Examples | Typical response |
|---|---|---|
| **Minor** | Off-topic drop, low-quality spam, accidental personal info in a note | Content removed, in-app notice |
| **Moderate** | Repeated spam, mild profanity, misleading content | Content removed + 7-day suspension |
| **Major** | Slurs, harassment, sexual content, promoting illegal activity | Content removed + 30-day suspension or immediate ban |
| **Severe** | Credible threats, doxing, child-safety violations, coordinated abuse | Immediate permanent ban + reported to authorities where applicable |

Bans are enforced against your anonymous device token. Evading a ban by reinstalling, resetting tokens, or creating new accounts is itself a violation and will lead to further enforcement.

---

## Appeals

If you believe content was removed in error, or your account was suspended unfairly, email **safety@versedrop.app** with:

- A description of the action you're appealing
- Your anonymous device token (so we can find the record)
- Why you believe the action was incorrect

We will review and respond. Decisions on appeals are final.

## Special situations

### Memorials and difficult locations

You can drop verses at hospitals, funeral homes, cemeteries, accident sites, and similar places. We ask that you be thoughtful: drops at these locations are seen by people in vulnerable moments. Choose verses of comfort, not judgment.

### Houses of worship

Drops at churches, mosques, temples, synagogues, and other houses of worship are welcome. **Drops that mock or attack any faith tradition are not.** This applies even if you disagree with that tradition.

### Political and current-event drops

VerseDrop is not a political platform. Verses that take a position on a contested current event are likely to be removed, even if the verse itself is clean — because the context turns it into commentary. When in doubt, drop the verse with no custom message and let it speak for itself.

### Schools and campuses

Drops at or very near K-12 schools require extra care. Educational settings have specific safety expectations. Don't drop on school grounds without permission; don't drop content directed at students.

## Reporting urgent safety issues

| Situation | What to do |
|---|---|
| Imminent physical danger | Call your local emergency number (911 in the US). Then report to safety@versedrop.app. |
| Credible threat against a specific person | Report the drop in-app and email safety@versedrop.app with the drop ID. |
| Child-safety concern (CSAM, grooming) | Report immediately to safety@versedrop.app — we report to the **National Center for Missing & Exploited Children (NCMEC)** as required by law. |
| Suicide or self-harm content | Report in-app and consider sharing crisis-line resources. In the US: 988 (Suicide & Crisis Lifeline). |
